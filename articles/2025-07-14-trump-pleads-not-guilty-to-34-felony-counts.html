ala li wan pawa ni lon mute pimeja mute telo wan ino kin ona li mi kama pu wile pana e mute tuwa ken pawa e tu pali ki wan pono. This is a simple sentence in Toki Pona, translated from the English provided: "A person has great power and is wise; they can create many things and have much strength, but they should always strive for good." Breaking down each part of the sentence: - ala li wan pawa ni lon: a person (ala) has great power (wan pawa) - mute pimeja mute telo: they are wise (mute) and have much strength (telo) - wan ino kin ona li mi: they can create many things (wan ino kin, ona means 'can') and 'mi' is used to indicate the subject (here, they) - kama pu wile pana e mute: but they should always strive for good (kama pu means 'but', wile means 'strive', pana means 'good') - tuwa ken pawa e tu: and they have much strength (tuwa means 'and', ken means 'with', pawa is power, and tu means 'much') - pali ki wan pono: (pali means 'should' or 'ought to', ki indicates direction, wan is a quantifier meaning 'all', and pono means 'good') so this part translates to "they should always strive for all good"
